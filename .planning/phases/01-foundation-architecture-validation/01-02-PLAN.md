- Every claim cites a source

- Wikipedia is a starting point, follow its references

**## Script Output Structure**
Every script produces a Documentary Script with these sections:
1. Hook (0:00-1:30) -- Cold open, no preamble, drop viewer into the most unsettling moment
2. Context (1:30-5:00) -- Background, historical setting, key players
3. Acts (5:00-end) -- 4-7 acts with dramatic question, rising tension, transition hooks
4. Resolution -- What happened, what we know, what remains unknown
5. Metadata -- Word count, estimated runtime (150 words/min), act breakdown, source count

**## Voice Rules (Summary)**
Introduce with: "These rules are extracted from the full voice profile. Read @channel/voice-profile.md for complete examples."
1. Declarative factual claims -- State facts as facts. No "reportedly", "allegedly", "it is believed" when sourced.
2. No cheap sensationalism -- The horror is in the facts. Let the story do the work.
3. Third-person narrator -- Never first person. The narrator is invisible.
4. Cinematic pacing -- Short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph. White space is a tool.
5. Specific over general -- Names, dates, places. Never "a small town" when you know the town's name.

**## Writing Procedure**
Four steps:
- Step 1: Absorb Research [DETERMINISTIC] -- Read Research.md and entity_index.json, identify narrative arc, select 3-5 hooks
- Step 2: Outline [HEURISTIC] -- Structure into 4-7 acts, assign dramatic questions, plan hook and transitions
- Step 3: Draft [HEURISTIC] -- Write full script, apply voice rules, every claim traces to source, target 3,000-7,000 words
- Step 4: Self-Review [HEURISTIC] -- Check for modal qualifiers, sensationalism, vague references, unsourced claims; verify word count
